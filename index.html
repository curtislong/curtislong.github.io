"The song, "Moon River," is an original composition for the screen written by Henry Mancini and Johnny Mercer for Audrey Hepburn to sing in Breakfast at Tiffany's. Its significance is rooted to Mancini's childhood in the Southern United States. The opening stanza goes, "Move River, wider than a mile. I'm crossing you in style, someday." Where the Moon River stands in for untapped adventure and crossing it is sating wanderlust, I like to think I'm adrift on the water, letting the current take me to the next beauty. Here are some of my pictures from along the way."
